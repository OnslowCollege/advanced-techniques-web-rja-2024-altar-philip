Youth's mental Health

First off, what is Mental Illness? Mental illness is a condition in a person's mental wellbeing, causing disorder to their behavior and thinking capabilities. How it happens if a person were to develop mental illness, it would begin from a situation or problem that occurred whether it is at home with family, at school with friends or teachers, or at their workplaces with their co-workers, these places could be the start to where the spark is first lit up of a person developing mental illness. If the issues from where they first appeared does not get sorted out and is significantly affecting the personâ€™s mental health in a negative way, then there is a high possibility that the person may develop mental illness and they should go and seek professional help. If the problem gets solved or it does not bother the person, then they will have a low chance of developing mental illness however they should still check with someone as a precaution so it does not develop into something bigger later on.

If a person were to have mental illness, what would they be experiencing? Things that people are experiencing when having mental illness can be: Anxiety Disorders Bipolar Disorder Depression PTSD Schizofrenia Eating Disorders Disruptive Behavior and Dissocial Disorders Neurodevelopmental Disorders 1 in every 8 people worldwide is under a type of mental illness condition. If we put this into perspective, in 2019, 970 million people worldwide were living with a mental illness condition. In 2020, the count rose drastically because of COVID - 19, with initial reports which estimated a 26% and 28% increase in cases of anxiety and major depressive disorders in one year. Options that give effective treatment and prevention for these cases, most people do not have available access to these treatments, and many others experience stigma, discrimination and violation of human rights.
